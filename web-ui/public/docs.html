What the product actually does

Xmore is built around operator usefulness: signal generation, ranking, monitoring, and research support for a market where context, liquidity, and timing matter.

Signal Engine

Adaptive consensus instead of a static vote

Agent weights adapt from live evaluated results, recent alpha, sample quality, and drift. The system keeps learning which components are earning trust now.

Research Layer

Research grounded in real market context

Research resolves bilingual entities, prioritizes symbol-specific retrieval, and answers using internal documents, market news, pricing context, and product knowledge.

Macro Layer

Market context is part of the decision, not a side note

Rates, gold, FX, regime shifts, and daily changes are surfaced alongside the signal layer so users can understand what changed and why it matters.

Portfolio Tools

Signals connect to portfolios and forecasts

Users can move from ranked opportunities to scenario portfolios, monitor open ideas, and inspect forecast quality over time.

Execution

Built with cost and friction awareness

Expected edge is evaluated against transaction costs, liquidity realities, and risk controls. That keeps the product closer to tradeable reality.

Coverage

One bilingual operating surface

Arabic and English users can search, ask, and navigate the same system without losing entity resolution, context, or usability.

Why teams use it

The value is not more data. The value is reducing the time between market information and a defensible decision.

Speed

Find what changed today fast

The platform highlights meaningful signal shifts, portfolio forecast deltas, and macro moves so users can focus on changes, not re-read the whole market every morning.

Discipline

Separate conviction from noise

Calibrated confidence, ranking score, and freshness monitoring help distinguish strong setups from stale or weak ones.

Clarity

Make research conversational without losing rigor

Users can ask plain-language questions and still get structured answers tied to actual market entities, retrieval evidence, and current system context.
